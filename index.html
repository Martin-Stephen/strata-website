§ 01 Why Strata exists

Private credit in AU/NZ is a ~A$200B market with no retail rail.

Global private credit is heading past US$5 trillion by 2030. In AU/NZ it is ~A$200B and growing, but retail access is broken: A$500K wholesale minimums, single-manager products, or an ASX LIT wrapper with no compliance rail underneath. ASIC's REP 820 (Nov 2025) and the FMA's post-Du Val posture make it harder, not easier, for mid-tier managers to build retail distribution themselves.

Strata is the wrapper they plug into. The regulated layer — master trust, tokenised unit ledger, PIE tax engine, secondary market, compliance surveillance — sitting between licensed credit managers and retail channels.

§ 02 The product

One NAV cycle, every investor, every PIR automatically.

Strata's multi-rate PIE tax engine applies the correct Prescribed Investor Rate (10.5% / 17.5% / 28%) to every distribution, per investor, per fund — without a manager ever touching a reconciliation schedule.

§ 04 Scope

Strata is infrastructure. Not a fund.

Strata does not hold credit risk, originate loans, or compete with our customers. That is the whole point — it is what makes the wrapper safe to plug into, and what keeps every incentive aligned.

The wrapper

NZ MIS under the FMA sandbox, PIE tax wrapper, master trust that spawns sub-trusts on demand. Add a fund, duplicate a deed.

The ledger

Tokenised units as the unit-holder registry. Programmable transfer. Automated distributions. Secondary market inside NAV windows.

The surface

Retail distribution via a direct Strata app and channels (Sharesies in discussion, Snowball / Kernel / InvestNow scoping, HUB24 / Praemium adviser tier later).
