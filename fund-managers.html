Risks + mitigations

The questions we expect you to ask.

Pre-revenue platform Correct. The wrapper, master trust, sub-trust IP, and tokenised ledger are built and regulatorily positioned; the first live fund is still ahead of us. We are open about this — the 4–6 month onboarding is what gets us there together.
NZ regulatory risk post-Du Val ASIC REP 820 (Nov 2025) and the FMA's post-Du Val posture are tailwinds, not headwinds — for compliant rails. Strata's transparency-first design is built specifically for this bar. MERW counsel throughout.
Distribution concentration risk Direct Strata front-end is day-one. We don't depend on any single third-party channel. Sharesies dialogue runs in parallel. Snowball / Kernel / InvestNow available as alternates. ASX LIT feeder as the AU-retail shortcut.
Sub-trust isolation Each fund is ring-fenced. One fund's loan losses cannot touch another's NAV. MERW-designed, documented in the master-trust deed.
Data residency Your loan-book data stays with you. Strata pulls NAV + unit-holder data over authenticated APIs. No raw credit-file replication.
Brand control Your fund, your PDS, your brand to retail. Strata is named only as the wrapper operator, never as a distributor or adviser.

Frequently asked

Questions we get in the first call.

Do we need an NZ MIS licence of our own?

No. Strata is the NZ-licensed MIS operator. You bring your AU AFSL (or NZ equivalent) and your fund; we provide the NZ wrapper. The manager's own regulatory surface is unchanged.

Does Strata hold any of our loan-book data?

No raw credit files. Strata receives NAV values, distribution schedules, and unit-holder data over authenticated APIs. The loan book stays in your infrastructure, under your custodian.

Can we brand the retail surface ourselves?

Yes. The direct Strata front-end displays your fund under your brand, with your PDS and your fee disclosures. Strata is credited as the wrapper operator in the footer of each fund page.

What does the secondary market look like for our unit-holders?

Peer-to-peer within NAV windows — typically weekly, unless you specify tighter. You set the liquidity rules per fund. Outside windows, units are held to next NAV.

How does the PIE tax engine actually work?

We handle the per-investor PIR (10.5% / 17.5% / 28%) assignment and apply it to each distribution automatically. The investor reads a clean year-end statement; you never reconcile a PIR schedule.

Can we onboard without an AU MIT in place?

Yes — NZ-first is the standard path. The AU MIT architecture is designed and MERW-memorandumed; we stand it up when commercial volume justifies it, typically post-launch.

What's the exclusivity posture?

Non-exclusive by default. You can continue to use your own distribution, ASX LITs, and other platforms. We'd rather be one of your channels than your only one.
