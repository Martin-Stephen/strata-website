Regulation & trust

NZ-regulated. AU MIT-portable. Built to the bar the regulator is raising.

Strata operates under a New Zealand Managed Investment Scheme licence and the FMA's Homeshare Exemption Notice 2025. The master-trust architecture, sub-trust deed, and tokenised unit ledger have been designed to meet ASIC REP 820's transparency and fair-dealing bar from day one.

What the regulation covers

Strata's surface vs the issuer's surface.

Strata's FMA licence covers the wrapper. It does not cover the underlying credit origination. Each side has its own regulatory surface — and its own responsibilities.

Strata's regulated surface

Scheme operationRegulated under NZ MIS + FMA sandbox
Unit issuanceTokenised units issued per sub-trust deed
AdministrationNAV, distributions, PIE tax, secondary market
Fair-dealingDisclosure, advertising, complaints — all audited
ReportingInvestor statements, regulator packs, audit evidence
CustodianRegulated custody arrangement per fund

Issuer's regulated surface

Credit originationIssuer's AFSL / MIS, not Strata's
Borrower underwritingIssuer's credit team, not Strata's
Loan-book managementIssuer's infrastructure, not Strata's
PDS contentIssuer-drafted, MERW-reviewed
Fund performanceIssuer-reported, Strata-published
Capital calls / redemptionsIssuer-set liquidity rules

Disclosures

Plain-language, available on request.

Risk statement

Every fund listed on Strata carries capital-loss risk. Private credit is not a term deposit. Past performance is not indicative. Full risk statement available on each fund's detail page when published.

Complaints + dispute resolution

Strata is registered with a NZ approved dispute-resolution scheme. Complaints are acknowledged within two business days and resolved within 20 business days where possible.

Conflicts of interest

Strata does not hold credit risk, originate loans, or compete with our customers. Our commercial model — setup fee + SaaS + AUA bps + transaction fees — is fully disclosed to every issuer.

AML / CFT

Strata performs its own KYC and AML checks on retail investors. Issuers retain their own AML obligations for borrowers. Reporting to the NZ FIU is automated.

Data residency

Retail investor PII is stored in NZ. NAV and distribution data flows over authenticated APIs. Issuer loan-book data never enters Strata's infrastructure.

Audit

Annual financial audit. Separate compliance audit by The Compliance Company. Full audit evidence available to institutional partners under NDA.
