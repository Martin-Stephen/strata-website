Frequently asked

Questions we get from the waitlist.

What is private credit, in plain English?

Private credit is lending done outside banks and outside the public bond market. A credit manager pools investor money and lends it to businesses that need debt finance — typically for property development, asset purchase, or growth capital. Investors get income from the loan repayments.

What's different about Strata?

Today in NZ, most private credit is only accessible to wholesale (A$500K+) investors or through a single manager's own product. Strata is a regulated rail that lets licensed credit managers offer their funds to retail investors — with proper disclosure, a PIE tax wrapper, and a secondary market.

Why a waitlist and not a live product?

The infrastructure (the wrapper, the master trust, the tokenised ledger) is built. The first fund on the rails is not yet live. We'd rather tell you honestly than pretend. When the first fund launches, waitlist members hear first.

Will Strata share my details with credit managers or channel partners?

Not without your explicit consent. Waitlist data is used only to email you about Strata product launches. Our privacy policy sets out the full detail.

Is there a minimum investment?

Final minimums are set per fund by the credit manager. Strata is engineered to support minimums below NZ$1,000 — the point of the rail is sub-wholesale access.

How are distributions taxed?

Through the NZ PIE regime. Your distributions are taxed at your Prescribed Investor Rate (10.5%, 17.5%, or 28%) depending on your income bracket — automatically, by Strata. No year-end reconciliation.

Can I sell my units early?

Yes — within NAV guardrail windows set by each fund. Strata operates a peer-to-peer secondary market. Liquidity is not guaranteed and depends on buyer demand at the time.

What are the risks?

Capital loss, illiquidity, credit concentration, operational risk, regulatory change. Private credit is riskier than bank deposits. Read the PDS for each fund, and consider talking to a licensed financial adviser.
